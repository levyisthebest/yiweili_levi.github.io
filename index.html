About

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate at the University of Georgia, advised by Prof. Tianming Liu. I earned my B.S. in Information Engineering from the Chien-Shiung Wu College at Southeast University, and my M.S. in Computer Science at Southeast University, where I worked with Prof. Hanchuan Peng on computational neuroscience.

My research sits at the intersection of AGI and computational neuroscience, with the goal of bridging machine intelligence and the human brain through multimodality. Recently I focus on Vision-Language Models (VLMs), World Models, and Eye-Gaze–guided learning, with strong interests in AI4Med and AI4Sci. I am currently a remote data scientist intern at CAMCA, Harvard Medical School.

My work spans:

  • Vision-Language Models & Reasoning: reference-based anatomical understanding with VLMs (RAU), eye-gaze guided medical VLMs, and quantum-adapted medical VLMs (MedQuA).
  • World Models & Video Generation: ECHOPulse — ECG-controlled echocardiogram video generation.
  • Eye Gaze: eye-gaze guided multimodal alignment for medical representation learning.
  • Sim2Real & Robotics: ALDM-Grasping (robot sim2real).
  • Neuron Reconstruction: NRRS — refining backbone deviation in neuron reconstruction.
