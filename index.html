I am a fourth-year CS Ph.D. candidate at the University of Georgia, advised by Prof. Tianming Liu, and a remote data scientist intern at CAMCA, Harvard Medical School. Previously I earned my M.S. at Southeast University with Prof. Hanchuan Peng on computational neuroscience.

My research lives at the intersection of AGI and computational neuroscience — bridging machine intelligence and the human brain through multimodality. These days that means Vision-Language Models, World Models, and Eye-Gaze–guided learning, with a strong pull toward AI4Med and AI4Sci.

"Bridge the gap between machine intelligence and the human brain."
